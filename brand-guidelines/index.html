01 / About

A working reference, not a museum piece.

This document sets out how Lutra Connect looks, sounds, and behaves in the world. It exists so that anyone producing something under the brand (a slide, a website, a sales sheet, a referral notification) gets it right the first time.

Read it once end to end. After that, use the contents list. The rules here are firm where they need to be (logo, colour, type, code) and explanatory where judgement is involved (voice, imagery). If a use case isn't covered, follow the spirit of what's here. If in doubt, ask.

02 / Brand essence

What Lutra Connect is, and why it exists.

Eye care in the UK runs on two professions that don't meet properly. Optometrists see patients first, on the high street. Ophthalmologists treat them, in NHS hospitals and private clinics. Between them sits a space that's informal, one-way, and unauditable: WhatsApp threads, faxed referrals, phone tag, favours called in between consultants who happen to know each other.

It works, until it doesn't. The cost is paid in lost information, delayed treatment, missed follow-ups, and clinical risk that nobody officially owns.

Lutra Connect is the connective layer that's been missing. Secure, structured, two-way, and auditable from the first message to the final outcome.

A consultant in every optometry practice. Sort of.

This is the line we lead with. Not a virtual consultant. A real one, vetted, indemnified, on the other end of a secure thread. Optometrists ask. Ophthalmologists answer. Patients get faster, better-informed care from the clinician they already trust.

Compliance is the mission, not the footnote.

Most of eye care's informal channels are regulatory liabilities sitting in clinicians' pockets. If you've shared a patient image on WhatsApp, the ICO considers that a personal data breach, and the clinician is the data controller. Connect replaces that with a secure, encrypted, audit-logged channel. Every clinician is vetted by us. Every message is attributable. Every action is defensible. This is what makes a clinical marketplace possible at all.

Three users, not two.

Connect is built for optometrists, for ophthalmologists, and for patients. Patients used to have two options when their optometrist flagged something: wait months on an NHS list, or pay several hundred pounds for a private hospital consultation. Connect gives them a third path. Fast, vetted specialist input delivered through the optometrist they already know, at a fraction of the cost. That third user is what turns a referral tool into an eye care pathway.

One place, from referral to outcomes.

Every interaction, every image, every piece of clinical data, every outcome, all in one place. Not because we want a moat, but because that's what defensible, longitudinal eye care actually requires. The audit trail, the clinical record, and the patient story end up in the same system.

The evidence

NHS Tayside ran the platform across 11 optometry practices and 1,570 patients over 29 months. Independently verified by NHS Tayside Business Intelligence: 8x ROI, referral-to-treatment time cut by 25%, 86% of patients listed directly for surgery, 4.7 out of 5 patient satisfaction. It works. The job now is scale.

How we win

Eye care won't be fixed by mandate. It will be fixed by tools clinicians actually want to use, and that pay for themselves on both sides of the referral. Usability and revenue. Not policy.

03 / Logo

Mark, wordmark, and lockup.

The Lutra Connect logo system is built around three elements. The mark (two overlapping navy discs). The wordmark ("lutra connect"). And the lockup, where the two sit together as the brand signature.

Primary lockup

The lockup is the default in almost every case. Use the on-light version against white or pale-lilac backgrounds. Use the on-navy version against any dark Lutra surface. Both are interchangeable in meaning; the rule is contrast.

Primary, on light Navy + lilac

Full specimen variants (on-navy, mono, dot, heavy, pill, plate) are included in the asset pack. The on-light primary lockup shown here is the default and should be reached for first.

Mark on its own

The mark stands alone in small spaces: favicons, app icons, social avatars, single-line UI elements. At very small sizes, prefer the mark over the full lockup.

04 / Clearspace & sizing

Give the mark room to breathe.

Maintain a clear margin around the lockup equal to the height of one disc of the mark. Nothing competes for attention inside that margin: no text, no graphics, no other logos, no edges.

Minimum size

For the full lockup, don't go below 96px wide on screen, or 24mm wide in print. Below that, switch to the mark or the heavy variant. The mark on its own works down to 16px (favicon territory).

05 / Parent & sibling

Lutra Connect is a product within Lutra Health.

Not a separate brand. The two marks share a parentage and should always feel related when they appear together.

Lutra Health
Lutra Connect

Pairing rules

  • Never overlap the two marks. If both must appear together, separate them with at least the width of a Health mark.
  • Never re-colour the Connect mark. Connect is always navy (or white inverse). It doesn't take on the Health mark's blue, or any other variation.
  • Match by visual weight. If the Health mark appears at height h, the Connect mark appears at height h as well. Match optical weight, not bounding-box height.
  • One at a time wherever possible. Connect stands alone on product surfaces. Health appears in footers, legal lines, or parent-company contexts.

Where each lives

  • Lutra Connect on product surfaces, sales materials, user-facing UI, and marketing for the product itself.
  • Lutra Health on corporate communications, investor materials, footers and legal lines on Connect surfaces, and parent-company contexts.
06 / Misuse

Don't.

The logo files supplied are the logo. Don't redraw, reweight, recolour, restructure, or restyle them. A few of the most common mistakes:

  • Stretch or distort the proportions.
  • Recolour the logo outside the brand palette.
  • Rotate, tilt, or set the logo on an angle.
  • Add drop shadows, glows, or other effects.
  • Place the logo on a background with insufficient contrast.
  • Crowd the logo. Honour the clearspace rule.

Proportions

Navy and white do most of the work. Lilac softens. Blue and teal are accents, used to draw the eye where it matters. As a rough guide: 60% white, 25% navy, 10% lilac, 5% blue or teal. Avoid full-bleed teal or blue surfaces; they're too loud as backgrounds.

09 / Voice

How we sound.

Candid, evidence-led, plainspoken. We name problems where we see them. We don't oversell. We talk to clinicians the way clinicians talk to each other: precise, time-respectful, allergic to fluff.

Three principles

  • Evidence over assertion. Our claims have published numbers behind them, or we don't make them.
  • Connect, don't replace. Both sides have invested in their own systems. We make those systems talk to each other.
  • Compliance is a design input. GDPR, NHS DSPT, MDR, ISO standards: these shape the product from day one, not bolted on at the end.

What we sound like, what we don't

We sound like
  • A senior colleague describing what works and what doesn't.
  • British English. Plain, unfussy, precise.
  • Numbers with sources behind them.
  • Short sentences mixed with longer ones. The occasional fragment.
  • "NHS Tayside cut referral time by 25%."
We don't sound like
  • A marketing department. No "transform", "unlock", "leverage", "seamless", "robust", "revolutionise".
  • An AI. No em dashes, no filler openers, no "Moreover".
  • A press release. No empty superlatives, no three-item lists when two would do.
  • "Dramatically improves efficiency."

What we are not

  • Not a referral management system that locks clinicians in.
  • Not another EPR.
  • Not a telehealth product.
  • Not a project that asks clinics to redesign their workflow before they see value.
10 / Imagery

Clinical, real, considered.

Actual environments over stock. People at work over people smiling at cameras. Consulting rooms, high-street practices, hospital clinics. Equipment, screens, hands. The texture of the job.

What good looks like

  • Available light. Avoid hard flash and over-staged setups.
  • Considered framing. Negative space is welcome.
  • Real people from the clinical community, where possible. Otherwise, restrained editorial-style stock.
  • Subjects looking at their work, not the camera.

What to avoid

  • Cliché clinical stock: gloved hands holding red apples, doctors pointing at iPads, eye-chart close-ups with rainbow gradients.
  • Patient-identifying imagery without explicit consent.
  • Heavy filters, tilt-shift effects, or AI-generated faces.
11 / CSS variables

For the design system.

Add these to the design system's tokens file under a /* Lutra Connect */ section. They reuse the Lutra Health primitives where they exist and layer on the Connect-specific values.

Colour tokens

/* ---------- Lutra Connect: colour ---------- */
--lutra-navy:           #001f54;   /* primary anchor */
--lutra-blue:           #2c48ed;   /* action */
--lutra-teal:           #01c7a9;   /* signal */
--lutra-lilac:          #eeedf4;   /* pale surface, shared w/ Health */

--connect-mark:         var(--lutra-navy);
--connect-mark-inverse: #ffffff;
--connect-pill:         #c5c0de;   /* deeper lilac, reads on white */
--connect-pill-on-navy: var(--lutra-lilac);
--connect-pill-fg:      var(--lutra-navy);

Typography tokens

/* ---------- Lutra Connect: type ---------- */
--font-display: 'Manrope', system-ui, sans-serif;
--font-body:    'Inter',   system-ui, sans-serif;
--font-mono:    ui-monospace, 'SF Mono', Menlo, monospace;

Usage example

/* Lilac pill, as used in "connect" wordmark contexts */
.connect-pill {
  background: var(--connect-pill);
  color:      var(--connect-pill-fg);
  border-radius: 0.28em;
  padding: 0.12em 0.26em 0.14em;
  font-family: var(--font-display);
  font-weight: 700;
}

Maintained alongside the Lutra Health design system. New tokens or token changes: raise an issue in the design-system repo.

13 / Files

All Lutra Connect logo assets are supplied in three formats: SVG (vector, for anything that supports it), PNG at standard resolution, and PNG at 2× for Retina or large-format use.

  • /png/: raster PNG at ~1200px wide, transparent background.
  • /png@2x/: same files at 2× for high-DPI displays and large print.
